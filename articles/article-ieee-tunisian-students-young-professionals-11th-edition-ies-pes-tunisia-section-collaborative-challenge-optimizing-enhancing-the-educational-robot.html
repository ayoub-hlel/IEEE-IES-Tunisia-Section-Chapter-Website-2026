As part of the 11th edition of the Tunisian Students & Young Professionals Congress (TSYP 11), the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society (IES) Tunisia Section Chapter and the IEEE Power & Energy Society (PES) Tunisia Section Chapter collaborated to present an exciting technical challenge: Optimizing & Enhancing The Educational Robot. This competition provided students and young professionals with an opportunity to design and develop a functional educational robot from the ground up, applying their technical expertise and innovative thinking.

Participants were tasked with conceptualizing and building a robot tailored for educational purposes. The challenge encouraged teams to explore creative designs, integrate smart functionalities, and ensure practical usability. Competitors had to address various engineering aspects, including mechanical construction, electronic control, software implementation, and energy efficiency.

By hosting this challenge, IEEE IES and PES Tunisia Section Chapters aimed to inspire the next generation of engineers and innovators. The competition served as a platform for experimentation, hands-on learning, and pushing the boundaries of educational robotics, making it one of the key highlights of TSYP 11.
