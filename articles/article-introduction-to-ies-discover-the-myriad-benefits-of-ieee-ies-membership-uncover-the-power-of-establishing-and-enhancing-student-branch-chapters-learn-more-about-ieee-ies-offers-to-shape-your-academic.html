During the 11th edition of the Tunisian Students & Young Professionals Congress (TSYP 11), Antonio Luque past director of region 8 & vice president for membership activities of the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society and Ala Chalghaf IES Student Activities Committe Chair delivered an insightful keynote on the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society (IES). This session provided attendees with a comprehensive overview of the society’s mission, activities, and the numerous benefits of IEEE IES membership.

Participants had the opportunity to explore the wealth of resources available through IES, including access to cutting-edge research, networking with industry experts, and opportunities to apply for scholarships and awards. The speakers also highlighted how students can actively engage with IES by participating in conferences, technical contests, and mentoring programs, fostering both academic and professional growth.

A key aspect of the discussion was the role of Student Branch Chapters in enhancing leadership skills and creating vibrant communities within IEEE. Attendees learned how establishing and strengthening these chapters can provide a structured pathway for collaboration, innovation, and career development. The session left participants inspired to take advantage of the many opportunities IEEE IES offers, reinforcing the society’s impact on the next generation of engineers and researchers.
