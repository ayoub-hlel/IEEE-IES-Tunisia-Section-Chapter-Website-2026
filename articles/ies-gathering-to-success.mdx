The IEEE Industrial Electronics Society (IES) Tunisia Chapter organized a comprehensive educational event on August 10, 2025, at the Institut International de Technologie (IIT) Sfax. The "IES Gathering to Success" brought together students, academic professionals, and industry practitioners for a full day of technical presentations, workshops, and networking opportunities.

## Event Overview and Objectives

Registration began at 8:30 AM, with participants from universities across Tunisia attending the event. The primary goal was to introduce students to the IES organization while providing practical guidance on career development in industrial electronics and automation technologies.

The opening session established the event's central theme: industrial electronics as a field that combines technical expertise with leadership opportunities through international collaboration and student chapter development.

## Technical Presentations and Industry Perspectives

The morning session featured a panel discussion with nine industry professionals and academics: Hadil Drisii, Amira Tmar, Ahmed Aouididi, Mohamed Amine Bouaziz, Mohamed Rebai, Mohamed Ali Krichen, Mariem Dhouib, Ahmed Borcheni, and Madiha Neifar. Each panelist presented their professional background, current research focus, and practical advice for students entering the field.

The presentations covered current industry trends, research opportunities in industrial electronics, and the practical challenges of transitioning from academic study to professional practice. Panelists emphasized the importance of hands-on project experience and international networking in building successful careers.

## Practical Workshops and Skill Development

The workshop sessions addressed four key areas of industrial electronics: smart systems implementation, robotics applications, renewable energy technologies, and Industry 4.0 integration. Each workshop included both theoretical background and practical exercises, allowing participants to work with relevant technologies and tools.

Participants rotated through different workshop stations, gaining exposure to various specializations within industrial electronics. The hands-on format enabled direct interaction between students and workshop leaders, facilitating detailed technical discussions.

## Student Chapter Development Initiative

A significant portion of the afternoon program focused on the practical aspects of establishing IES Student Branch Chapters at participating universities. The presentation outlined the formal requirements for chapter formation, available resources from the international IES organization, and funding opportunities for student activities.

Representatives from the IES Tunisia Chapter provided detailed information about the application process, ongoing support available to new chapters, and examples of successful student projects from existing chapters worldwide. Several attendees expressed immediate interest in pursuing chapter establishment at their respective institutions.

## Professional Networking and Collaboration Opportunities

The event structure incorporated multiple networking opportunities, including scheduled breaks and informal discussion periods. These sessions facilitated connections between students from different universities and enabled direct access to industry professionals and researchers.

Many participants exchanged contact information and discussed potential collaborative projects. The networking component proved particularly valuable for students seeking research supervisors, internship opportunities, and project partners.

## Outcomes and Future Planning

By the conclusion of the event, attendees had gained a comprehensive understanding of career paths in industrial electronics, practical knowledge of current technologies, and clear guidance on professional development opportunities. Multiple students committed to initiating the chapter formation process at their universities.

The event successfully achieved its objectives of increasing awareness of the IES organization, providing practical technical education, and establishing a network of engaged students across Tunisia's engineering programs.

## Conclusion

The "IES Gathering to Success" demonstrated the effectiveness of combining educational content with practical networking opportunities. The event provided valuable resources for students while strengthening the IES presence in Tunisia's academic community. The success of this initiative suggests strong potential for expanding IES student activities throughout the region.

Future events will build on this foundation, continuing to connect students with industry professionals while promoting technical excellence and international collaboration in industrial electronics.
